# HomeGuard™ Code & Standards Reference
*Research backing for every claim and recommendation in the HomeGuard™ Guide,
Pro reminder emails, storm notices, and site content. Compiled June 2026.*

**How to use this file:** each entry maps a HomeGuard claim → the governing code,
standard, or authority. Where our advice exceeds code minimums, that's noted —
"best practice" and "code minimum" are different things, and we say which is which.

**Code editions note (verify before quoting in new content):**
- **Maryland** enforces the **Maryland Building Performance Standards (MBPS)**,
  COMAR 09.12.51, which adopts the **2021 IBC / IRC / IECC** with state
  amendments. MD law requires updating to each new ICC edition on a rolling
  cycle, and **counties may amend** everything except the energy and
  accessibility codes (those may only be made stricter).
- **Washington, DC** enforces the **2017 DC Construction Codes** (Title 12 DCMR):
  the **2015 I-Codes + 2014 NEC** with DC amendments, including the **DC Property
  Maintenance Code**. A newer edition has been in development; check
  dob.dc.gov before citing section numbers in print.
- "International" codes (IBC/IRC/IPMC etc.) are the **ICC model codes** — the
  basis for nearly all US state/local codes. Other countries use different
  systems (Eurocodes in the EU, the National Building Code in Canada); HomeGuard
  targets US/DMV practice.

## 1. Water management & drainage

| HomeGuard claim | Backing |
|---|---|
| "Grading should fall ~6 inches over the first 10 feet" (Guide: grading; March Pro email) | **IRC R401.3 Drainage** — grade shall fall ≥6" within the first 10 ft of the foundation; where barriers prevent it, drains or swales are required. Impervious surfaces within 10 ft must slope ≥2% away. |
| "Downspouts should discharge 4–6 ft from the foundation" (Guide; April/Flood notices) | **IRC R801.3 Roof drainage** — requires discharge ≥5 ft from foundation walls (or to an approved drainage system) where expansive/collapsible soils exist. Our 4–6 ft advice applies it everywhere as best practice; parts of MD/NoVA do have marine clay (expansive) soils. |
| "Test your sump pump; don't route it to the sewer" (April/Aug emails; flood notice) | **IRC P3303 Sumps and Pumping Systems** (discharge piping, check valve); **WSSC Plumbing & Fuel Gas Code** (Montgomery & Prince George's): sump discharge to the **sanitary sewer is prohibited**. |
| "Gutter pitch should move water to downspouts" (Guide glossary; Oct email) | Industry standard (~1/4" per 10 ft); gutters are not directly specified in the IRC for slope — best-practice claim, correctly not framed as code. |
| "Clean window-well debris before floods" (Flood notice) | **IRC R310.2** (emergency escape window wells must remain operational; ladder required where well depth >44"). Keeping wells clear preserves both drainage and egress. |

## 2. Roof, attic & exterior envelope

| HomeGuard claim | Backing |
|---|---|
| "Attic ventilation failure cooks shingles / raises bills" (June/July emails; Guide soffit entry) | **IRC R806.2 Minimum vent area** — net free ventilating area ≥1/150 of the attic area (1/300 with balanced high/low vents + conditions). |
| "Ice dams = heat escaping the attic" (Guide glossary; Jan/snow notices) | Consequence of R806 ventilation + **IECC ceiling insulation requirements**; ice-dam physics per FEMA/Building Science guidance. Correctly framed as diagnosis, not code violation. |
| "Mulch piled against siding is a termite bridge / keep ~6 inches clear" (Guide p.16; May email) | **IRC R317.1** — wood siding/sheathing/framing with <6" clearance from the ground must be decay-protected; **IRC R318** — subterranean termite protection methods. UMD Extension repeats the 6" rule for mulch. |
| "Termite swarmers: same-size wings = termites; get an inspection" (May email) | **University of Maryland Extension**, "Ants and Termites: How to Tell the Difference" — equal wings, straight antennae, no waist = termite. |
| "Flashing directs water at chimneys/valleys" (Guide glossary; April/Oct emails) | **IRC R903.2 / R905** flashing requirements at walls, chimneys, valleys. |
| "Drip edge guides water into gutters" (Guide glossary) | **IRC R905.2.8.5** — drip edge **required** at eaves and rakes of shingle roofs. |

## 3. Life safety (the highest-stakes section)

| HomeGuard claim | Backing |
|---|---|
| Smoke alarms on every level | **IRC R314** (every bedroom, outside sleeping areas, every story incl. basements); **Maryland Public Safety §9-104**: every level required in ALL homes; battery-only replacements must be **sealed 10-year units with hush feature** (since 2018); units >10 yrs from manufacture date must be replaced. **DC Code §6-751.02** + DC Property Maintenance Code: same coverage in DC. |
| "Test alarms; replace units at 10 years" (Sept email — *corrected, see §7*) | **NFPA 72** (replace at 10 years); **CPSC**: test monthly. |
| CO alarms outside sleeping areas if fuel appliances/attached garage | **IRC R315**; **Maryland Public Safety Title 12, Subtitle 11** (pre-2008 homes with fuel appliance/fireplace/attached garage: every level + outside sleeping areas); **DC Property Maintenance Code** (all dwellings with a CO source). UL 2034 listing required. |
| "Space heaters: wall outlet, never a power strip; 3 ft clearance" (Jan email) | **NFPA 1 §11.5** (direct connection); **UL 1278** listing instructions; NFPA "3-foot rule" for anything that can burn. |
| "Chimney: book a sweep before first cold snap" (Sept email) | **NFPA 211 §14.2** — chimneys, fireplaces and vents **shall be inspected at least annually** (Level 1 minimum) and cleaned/repaired as needed. |
| "GFCI test buttons monthly" (Feb email) | **NEC (NFPA 70) 210.8** requires GFCI protection (kitchens, baths, garages, exterior, basements); monthly TEST/RESET per **CPSC GFCI Fact Sheet #99** and UL 943 manufacturer instructions. |
| "Dryer vent: clean to the exterior hood" (May email) | **IRC M1502** — ducts terminate outside, **no screens** at termination, backdraft damper required, 35-ft max equivalent length, 4" metal duct. Lint = leading cause of dryer fires (NFPA). |
| "Never run a generator in a garage" (Ice-storm notice) | **CPSC** CO guidance; generator manufacturer instructions (UL 2201). |
| "Live tree fires / water daily" (Dec email) | **NFPA** Christmas tree fire data — dry-tree fires are among the fastest, deadliest home fires. |
| "Water heater: flush sediment; 120°F" (Feb email) | Manufacturer maintenance schedules (flush); **CPSC Pub. 5098** — 120°F setting prevents scalds (~1,500 hospitalizations/yr). Framed as maintenance guidance, not code. ✔ |

## 4. Hiring contractors (Contractor Clarity™, Red Flag Checklist, blog)

| HomeGuard claim | Backing |
|---|---|
| "Verify the license" (Red Flag Checklist; verify-contractor-license post) | **Maryland**: MHIC license required for home improvement work ≥$500 (**Bus. Reg. §8-601**); verify at labor.maryland.gov. **DC**: Home Improvement Contractor license + **$25,000 bond** (16 DCMR ch. 8); verify via DOB. |
| "Demands full payment upfront = red flag" (Red Flag Checklist; never-pay-cash post) | **Maryland Bus. Reg. §8-617**: contractor may not accept **more than 1/3 of the contract price** as deposit, and nothing before the contract is signed. **DC 16 DCMR §800.1**: an **unlicensed** person may not accept ANY payment before full completion. So in both jurisdictions, "half down in cash today" is illegal or a licensing red flag — stronger than our copy even claims. |
| "Storm chasers: don't sign at the door; you can cancel" (storm-chaser post) | **FTC Cooling-Off Rule, 16 CFR 429**: 3 business days to cancel door-to-door sales ≥$25. **Maryland Door-to-Door Sales Act (Com. Law §14-301 et seq.)** + MHIC: for home improvement contracts solicited at the home, **5 business days** to cancel — **7 days if the homeowner is 65+**. |
| "Avoids permits = red flag" (Red Flag Checklist) | Permits are required for most structural/electrical/plumbing work in every DMV jurisdiction. Example: **Montgomery County DPS requires a building permit for ALL residential decks regardless of height**. The party pulling the permit is the party responsible — a contractor pushing the homeowner to pull it is shifting liability. |
| "Pre-1978 home? Painting/renovation disturbs lead paint" (resources) | **EPA RRP Rule, 40 CFR 745 subpart E**: firms disturbing paint in pre-1978 homes must be **EPA Lead-Safe Certified**. Most DC rowhouses and a large share of MD housing stock are pre-1978. |
| "A written contract with license number" (contract tips) | **MD Bus. Reg. §8-501** + MHIC contract rules: written, legible contract with name, address, MHIC number. |

## 7. Corrections made & caveats to keep

1. **September Pro email — FIXED.** It said "replace smoke and CO detector
   batteries." Under MD Public Safety §9-104, battery smoke alarms must now be
   sealed 10-year units — there are no batteries to replace. Copy now says:
   test alarms, check the manufacture date, replace units over 10 years old
   (sealed 10-year units required in MD).
2. **Downspout distance**: if we ever say "code requires 5 feet," add "in
   expansive-soil areas" or keep it as best practice. Current copy is fine.
3. **Edition drift**: MD moves to newer I-codes on a cycle; DC has a new code
   edition pending. This file cites sections stable across 2015–2024 editions,
   but re-verify before printing section numbers in customer-facing material.
4. **"$5,000 repair" style claims** in the guide are illustrative, not sourced.
   Acceptable as marketing, but don't attach them to specific code citations.
5. **Local variation is real**: counties amend the MBPS. Where copy says "your
   county may require a permit," keep that phrasing — it's the accurate one.
