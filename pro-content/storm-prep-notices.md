## 1. Severe Thunderstorm / High Wind (spring–summer)
**Subject:** ⚠️ Storm prep: 3 things before tonight's wind
**Send when:** Severe thunderstorm watch or wind advisory (45+ mph gusts).

{{first_name}} — strong storms are forecast for our area. Three things, 15 minutes:

1. **Bring in or anchor anything that flies:** trash cans, umbrellas, cushions, grills. Wind damage from your own yard isn't always covered.
2. **Check that downspout extensions are attached** and pointing away from the house.
3. **Park away from large trees** if you can.

After it passes: walk the perimeter (guide's Small Habit Wins, p. 20). Look for lifted shingles, downed limbs touching the roof, and granules piled at downspout exits. Photograph anything off and log it.

## 2. Hurricane / Tropical Remnants (Aug–Oct)
**Subject:** ⚠️ {{storm_name}} prep: do these today, not tomorrow
**Send when:** Tropical storm watch or remnant rainfall event forecast.

{{first_name}} — {{storm_name}} is tracking toward the region with heavy rain and gusts expected {{timing}}. Today:

1. **Test the sump pump** (bucket of water in the pit). If it fails, get a backup plan tonight — even a utility pump and a hose.
2. **Clear gutters and downspout exits.** Six hours of tropical rain finds every clog.
3. **Charge phones and batteries; refill prescriptions** if you're low.
4. **Move valuables off basement floors** — 30 minutes of lifting beats a ruined photo box.
5. **Film a quick walkthrough** of your home for insurance documentation.

We'll be doing post-storm inspections across MD & DC after it passes. Reply if you need eyes on something.

## 3. Ice Storm / Freezing Rain (Dec–Feb)
**Subject:** ⚠️ Ice storm prep: pipes, power, and one faucet trick
**Send when:** Ice storm warning or freezing rain advisory.

{{first_name}} — ice is coming. Before tonight:

1. **Let one faucet drip** on any plumbing run along an exterior wall, and open the cabinets under those sinks.
2. **Know where your main water shutoff is** — if a pipe bursts, seconds matter. Go touch it now.
3. **Charge everything; locate flashlights.** Ice = downed lines in the DMV, almost guaranteed.
4. **Never run a generator in a garage,** even with the door open.

After: check ceilings under the roof and around chimneys for new stains — ice forces water sideways under shingles in ways rain never does.

## 4. Heavy Snow / Snow Load (Dec–Mar)
**Subject:** ⚠️ Snow prep: your roof and your furnace vent
**Send when:** Winter storm warning, 6"+ forecast.

{{first_name}} — real snow this time. Three things:

1. **Find your furnace's exterior intake/exhaust vents** (usually low on a side wall) and plan to keep them clear — blocked vents shut down heat or back up CO.
2. **Roof rake the first 3-4 feet from the gutter edge** after it stops, if you have one — that's where ice dams form.
3. **Don't chip ice off gutters.** You'll take the gutter (and shingle edge) with it. Heat cable or patience.

Watch for: icicles forming at specific spots = heat escaping there. Note the locations in your tracker for a spring insulation fix.

## 5. Flash Flood / Sustained Heavy Rain (any season)
**Subject:** ⚠️ Flood watch: basement triage in 20 minutes
**Send when:** Flash flood watch with 2"+ rain forecast.

{{first_name}} — serious rain is coming. The 20-minute basement drill:

1. **Sump pump test** — now, not when it's raining.
2. **Lift what matters:** anything cardboard or electrical goes up a shelf.
3. **Walk your downspouts** — every one should discharge 4+ feet out. A $12 extension tonight beats a wet basement tomorrow.
4. **Check window wells** for leaf buildup — they become aquariums in flash floods.

If water does get in: photograph before you clean up, then dry within 48 hours (fans + dehumidifier) to stay ahead of mold. Reply if you need a referral for water mitigation — we know who's honest.
