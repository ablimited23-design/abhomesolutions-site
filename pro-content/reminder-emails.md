## Email 1 — January
**Subject:** January check: the freeze is doing damage you can't see yet
**Preview:** Ice dams, pipes, and a 2-minute furnace filter check.

Hi {{first_name}}, here's your January 15-minute list:

- **Look up at your roofline after snow.** Icicles at the gutter edge = early ice dam. Note which sections — that's where heat is escaping your attic.
- **Open cabinets under sinks on exterior walls** during hard freezes; let warm air reach the pipes.
- **Check your furnace filter.** A clogged filter in January is the #1 cause of "my heat can't keep up."
- **Watch your windows for heavy condensation** — it means humidity is too high and feeding mold somewhere you can't see.
- **Space heaters:** plugged into the wall, never a power strip.

**Pro tip:** After the next melt, walk your basement perimeter and sniff. A musty corner in January tells you where water will come in this spring — flag it now in your tracker while it's cheap to fix.

Log it: [your trackers](https://abhomesolutions.pro/downloads/pro/homeguard-pro-trackers.pdf)

## Email 2 — February
**Subject:** February check: your house has been holding its breath for 3 months
**Preview:** Roof scan, water heater flush, and the GFCI test nobody does.

Hi {{first_name}}, February's 15-minute list:

- **From the ground, scan your roof** with fresh eyes (binoculars help): slipped shingles, lifted edges, sagging gutter sections from ice weight.
- **Flush a few gallons from your water heater** — sediment builds all winter and shortens its life.
- **Press "test" on every GFCI outlet** (kitchen, baths, garage, exterior). Takes 3 minutes.
- **Peek into the attic with a flashlight:** dark streaks on the underside of the roof deck = moisture getting in.
- **Check washing machine hoses** for bulges — they fail in year 5-8 and flood laundry rooms.

**Pro tip:** Book your spring HVAC service *now*. By April, every company in the DMV is slammed and charging peak rates.

## Email 3 — March
**Subject:** March check: winter's bill comes due this month
**Preview:** The post-freeze walk-around that catches 80% of spring problems.

Hi {{first_name}}, March's list — this is the big one:

- **Do the full exterior walk-around** (guide, page 11). Freeze-thaw just spent 3 months working on your caulk, concrete, and siding.
- **Check grading:** soil that settled over winter now slopes *toward* your foundation. Six inches of fall over ten feet is the target.
- **Clear gutters and downspout exits** of winter debris before April rain.
- **Look at every caulk line** around windows and doors — cracked or pulling away means water gets in during spring storms.
- **Put screens back, check weep holes** at window bottoms are clear.

**Pro tip:** Take photos of any concrete cracks today and again in May. Growing = call a pro. Static = monitor. Your tracker has a spot for both.

## Email 4 — April
**Subject:** April check: DMV storm season opens this month
**Preview:** Sump pump test, downspout extensions, flashing check.

Hi {{first_name}}, April's storm-readiness list:

- **Test your sump pump:** pour a bucket of water into the pit; it should kick on, pump out, and shut off. If you've never tested it, do this today.
- **Confirm downspouts discharge 4-6 feet from the foundation.** Extensions cost $12 and prevent the most common DMV basement leak.
- **Check roof flashing** around the chimney and valleys from the ground — that's where wind-driven rain enters.
- **Inspect your deck:** wobbly railings, soft boards, rusted connectors — before the season starts.
- **Walk the yard for widow-makers:** dead limbs over the house, driveway, or play areas.

**Pro tip:** When a storm warning hits, you'll get a Pro storm notice from us with the 3-things-before-it-hits list. Don't pre-worry — just keep the sump tested and the spouts extended.

## Email 5 — May
**Subject:** May check: test the AC before the first 90° day
**Preview:** Plus termite swarm season — what flying ants really mean.

Hi {{first_name}}, May's list:

- **Run your AC for 20 minutes today.** Weak cooling now = service call this week, not during the July heat wave when everyone's booked.
- **Termite swarmers are flying in Maryland right now.** Winged insects near windows or mulch beds? Same-size wings = termites, get an inspection. (Different size = flying ants, relax.)
- **Check exterior spigots for leaks** now that they're back in use — a drip against the foundation runs all summer.
- **Clean your dryer vent** all the way to the exterior hood.
- **Look under sinks and around toilets** — spring is when winter's slow drips show up as warped cabinet floors.

**Pro tip:** Mulch should sit 6 inches below your siding line. Piled against the house, it's a termite bridge and a moisture trap — the guide's Landscaping Traps page (16) has the full list.

## Email 6 — June
**Subject:** June check: the maple helicopters just clogged your gutters
**Preview:** Plus attic heat, pressure washing, and storm-settled grading.

Hi {{first_name}}, June's list:

- **Check gutters again** — spring seed pods and pollen mats clog them faster than fall leaves. Water overshooting the gutter in rain = clogged.
- **Feel your attic on a hot afternoon.** If it's brutal, your ventilation is failing — that cooks shingles from below and raises your cooling bill.
- **Pressure wash siding carefully** (low pressure, downward angle — never blast upward under laps).
- **Re-check grading and downspout exits** after spring storms moved soil around.
- **Trim vegetation 12 inches off the siding** for airflow.

**Pro tip:** While you're up checking gutters, photograph the roof. June photos are your "before" evidence if summer storms cause damage and you need an insurance claim.

## Email 7 — July
**Subject:** July check: your AC is running a marathon — help it out
**Preview:** Condenser coils, mystery water bills, and attic temps.

Hi {{first_name}}, July's list:

- **Hose off your AC condenser coils** (power off first). Cottonwood fluff and grass clippings choke airflow and add 10-20% to cooling costs.
- **Give the condenser 2 feet of clearance** — trim anything closer.
- **Check your water bill against last July.** A jump with no lifestyle change = a silent leak, usually a toilet flapper.
- **Ceiling fans counterclockwise** in summer (you should feel the breeze below).
- **Check window AC units' tilt** — they should drain outward, not into your wall.

**Pro tip:** On a 95° day, touch your ceiling under the attic. Hot to the hand = insulation or ventilation problem that's costing you every month. Flag it for fall — that's the cheap season to fix it.

## Email 8 — August
**Subject:** August check: hurricane remnant season — 30 minutes of insurance
**Preview:** Document your home before you need to prove what it looked like.

Hi {{first_name}}, August's list:

- **Walk every room and the exterior with your phone camera rolling.** Five minutes of video = proof of condition for any future claim. Store it in the cloud.
- **Photograph your roof, siding, and fence** specifically — the things storms damage.
- **Re-test the sump pump** (last test was April — that's too long ago).
- **Stage loose yard items** mentally: what gets thrown in the shed when a tropical remnant tracks our way?
- **Check tree limbs over the house** one more time — August storms drop them.

**Pro tip:** Save your insurance policy's declarations page to your phone today. After a storm, you want your coverage numbers and claim line in your pocket, not in a filing cabinet.

## Email 9 — September
**Subject:** September check: everything outside wants to live inside now
**Preview:** Seal the gaps, book the chimney sweep, furnace prep.

Hi {{first_name}}, September's list:

- **Walk the foundation line and seal gaps** bigger than a pencil — mice need only that much, and they're house-hunting this month.
- **Book furnace service now** (same logic as spring: October booking = November appointment).
- **Use a fireplace? Book the chimney sweep** before the first cold snap.
- **Test every smoke and CO alarm, and check the manufacture date on the back.** Units over 10 years old must be replaced — and in Maryland, replacement battery alarms must be sealed 10-year units with a hush button (it's the law, Public Safety §9-104).
- **Check weatherstripping:** close a door on a dollar bill; if it slides out easily, the seal is gone.

**Pro tip:** September is the DMV's best month for exterior work — contractors are between summer rush and fall panic, and caulk/paint cure perfectly in this weather. Anything you flagged all summer: fix it this month.

## Email 10 — October
**Subject:** October check: leaves are about to bury your gutters
**Preview:** Plus the last warm-weather window for caulk and concrete.

Hi {{first_name}}, October's list:

- **Plan two gutter cleanings:** one mid-October, one after the trees finish (mid-November). One cleaning isn't enough in the DMV.
- **Check roof valleys and flashing** while you're up there — that's where wet leaves pile up and rot.
- **Last call for exterior caulk and concrete crack filler** — both need 50°+ to cure right.
- **Walkway and step lights working?** Dark evenings start now; so do trip hazards.
- **Test your heat for real** — run it 30 minutes, check every room warms.

**Pro tip:** When you clean gutters, check the pitch: water should move toward downspouts, not pool. A sagging section now becomes an ice dam anchor in January.

## Email 11 — November
**Subject:** November check: winterize this weekend, thank yourself in January
**Preview:** Spigots, hoses, pipe insulation, and the draft hunt.

Hi {{first_name}}, November's list:

- **Disconnect hoses, shut off and drain exterior spigots** — the #1 preventable winter repair in our area. (Run water through them first per the guide's Small Habit Wins.)
- **Insulate pipes in unheated spaces:** garage, crawl space, that one cabinet on an exterior wall.
- **Reverse ceiling fans clockwise** to push warm air down.
- **Hunt drafts with a candle** on a windy day: window edges, outlet plates on exterior walls, the attic hatch.
- **Stock ice melt and a roof rake now** — they sell out the day before the first storm.

**Pro tip:** Your attic hatch is probably the biggest hole in your insulation. An insulated cover costs $30 and pays for itself this winter.
